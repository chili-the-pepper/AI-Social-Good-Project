# AI-Social-Good-Project
## Problem: Who's affected and what is the issue?
Students at SJSU often note the grievances regarding trashcans. This semester new trashcans replaced the previous ones, but students continued to find trashcans overfilled. They would stack their trash on top of existing trash because areas with high traffic recieved more trash. These trashcans aren't emptied more frequently than less used trashcans even though they should be.

## AI Capability: What addresses the failure point and why does it fit?
The campus doesn't have an efficient way of emptying trash cans around campus. Students often find them full or overflowing. AI addresses this problem by sending a report to a custodian for them to be able to locate the trash can and deal with whatever issue there is. AI specifically is useful because it allows for multiple reports to go through without need for manual reviwew of each one.

## Workflow: What goes in, what does the AI do, what comes out, and who acts on the output? 
Students or anyone on the SJSU campus will write a report on overfilled trashcans or other issues regarding the trashcans. The AI will recieve and analyze this report for the location of the trashcan, the specific issue stated, how urgent it is, and if there is any noted frequency of the reported issue. It will also weed out any obscure/nonsensical/unrelated reports. The AI's output will be sent to a campus custodian. The notification will be concise and display the summarized problem, location, frequency and urgency. This will allow campus custodians to know of high traffic trash areas and empty trashcans to reduce littering.

## Failure Case: An example of a failure to show failures are possible.
Someone could falsely report an off-campus location or use the reporting system for needs other than trash can emptying. There's also the possibility the AI recieves a vague report and doesn't understand the severity of the situation. 

## Oversight and Tradeoff: Where does human review sit, and what does the one change cost?
Human review sits with montoring reports to find initial errors and mistakes with the AI and reporting system. Humans need to ensure the system is being used correctly and outputting correctly.
